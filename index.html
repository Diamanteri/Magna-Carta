The Great Charter

The Magna Carta contained 63 clauses, but these 39 key provisions established foundational principles of liberty that would echo through centuries of legal and democratic development.

I

Clause 1 — The English Church shall be free

Anglicana ecclesia libera sit, et habeat jura sua integra...

Plain English: The Church operates without royal interference; context: medieval power balance.
II

Clause 2 — Inheritance and relief

Si quis comitum vel baronum nostrorum...

Established rules for inheritance and payments to the crown.
III

Clause 3 — Heirs and wardship

Si quis autem de talibus diem obierit...

Regulated guardianship of minor heirs and their estates.
IV

Clause 4 — Guardians must maintain property

Custos terre hujusmodi heredis...

Guardians must preserve and maintain inherited property.
V

Clause 5 — Guardians must account for revenues

Custos, quamdiu custodiam terre habuerit...

Guardians must account for revenues from the estate.
VI

Clause 6 — Heirs may marry without disparagement

Heredes maritentur absque disparagatione...

Heirs could not be forced into unsuitable marriages.
VII

Clause 7 — Widows' rights

Vidua post mortem mariti sui...

Protected widows' rights to property and remarriage.
VIII

Clause 8 — No forced marriage for widows

Nulla vidua distringatur ad se maritandum...

Widows could not be forced to remarry against their will.
IX

Clause 9 — Debt collection

Nos non distringemus nec capiemus...

Limited the crown's ability to seize property for debt.
X

Clause 10 — Jewish lending restrictions

Si quis mutuo ceperit aliquid a Judeis...

Regulated Jewish moneylending practices.
XI

Clause 11 — Debts to the crown

Et si quis mortuus fuerit, et debita...

Established procedures for settling debts to the crown.
XII

Clause 12 — Scutage and aid

Nullum scutagium vel auxilium ponatur in regno nostro...

Taxation required common consent of the realm.
XIII

Clause 13 — London's liberties

Et civitas Londoniarum habeat omnes antiquas libertates...

Confirmed the City of London's traditional liberties.
